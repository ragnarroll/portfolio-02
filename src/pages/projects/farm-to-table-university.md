### The health crisis is a food crisis

Our food industry is just that—an industry. Industrialized agriculture and food processing are linked to health epidemics and climate crises.

What would an educational solution look like? How could education help transform the food industry, building a better relationship between humans and the land that produces food?

### Our solution: A farm-to-table education model

We took inspiration from David Staley’s idea of a micro-college, and combined it with the actual micro-college called Deep Springs College, and the world-renowned sustainability leader Wageningen University.

Farm-to-table curriculum.
	Farm, food, and distribution

Micro-college, with multiple sources of income. 
Certificates, farms, philanthropy, grants, and tuition.

Students would play multi-faceted roles, helping with the administration of the school.
